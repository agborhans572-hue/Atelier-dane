Since 1987

A company built on the belief that furniture should last.

Dane Design was founded in Copenhagen by cabinetmaker Henrik Dahl with a single workshop, three apprentices, and an unshakeable conviction: that the world needed fewer, better things.

1987

Henrik Dahl opens the first Dane Design workshop in Bredgade, Copenhagen. The founding collection comprises twelve pieces.

1998

Collaboration begins with Swedish textile weavers. Natural fibre upholstery becomes a Dane Design signature, earning the brand its first Wallpaper* feature.

2008

FSC certification achieved across all timber sourcing. The sustainability charter is published — a commitment to material transparency that still guides every decision.

2025

Twelve Nordic workshop partnerships, 200+ signature pieces, and 40,000 homes furnished. The mission is unchanged: fewer things, made better.

Our Philosophy

Calm design for a complex world.

We design against noise — visual, material, and cultural. Each piece begins not with a sketch but with a question: what is this object, at its absolute simplest? The answer becomes the form.

Our process is slow by intent. A new piece typically spends eighteen months moving between paper, model, and prototype before it earns a place in the collection. Most ideas are discarded. The ones that remain are the ones that feel inevitable.

We do not follow trends. We do not design for photography. We design for rooms — for the way a chair occupies space at eight in the morning, and for the way it will look thirty years from now when it belongs entirely to whoever sits in it.

The People

The designers behind the work.

LS

Lars Svensson

Lead Designer · Copenhagen

40+ pieces in the collection. Lars trained under Danish furniture master Ole Wanscher and brings an architectural rigour to every form he creates. He is known for the steam-bent Aura chair and the Freya lounger.

IH

Ingrid Holm

Textile & Materials · Stockholm

Ingrid oversees all fabric and surface development in partnership with Scandinavian mills. Her palette work — anchored in undyed naturals and mineral tones — defines the Dane Design colour language.

MK

Mikkel Kjær

Lighting & Objects · Oslo

Mikkel joined in 2012 and has since developed the entire lighting collection. His approach treats light itself as a material — shaping pools and shadows as deliberately as a cabinetmaker shapes wood.

Where it's made

Twelve workshops. One standard.

Every Dane Design workshop is visited annually by our production team. We do not audit from a distance. We sit in the workshop, speak with the craftspeople, and inspect their tools and materials ourselves.

Denmark · Copenhagen

Dahl Snedkeri

Our founding workshop, established 1987. Specialises in steam-bent hardwood seating and hand-finishing. Seven master cabinetmakers.

Sweden · Gothenburg

Holm Väveri

Fourth-generation textile weavers. Produces all bouclé, linen, and wool upholstery fabrics using traditional floor looms.

Norway · Bergen

Fjord Metall

Hand-forged steel and brass components for lighting and table bases. All brass is solid, never plated.

Finland · Helsinki

Laine Puutyö

Solid birch and pine carcass work for storage. Responsible for the Boreas modular shelving system and all wardrobe frames.

Denmark · Aarhus

Kjær Glas

Hand-blown glass shades and panels. The Isvik chandelier globes are each individually blown in this workshop to a tolerance of ±2mm.

Sweden · Malmö

Nord Läder

Full-grain leather cutting and stitching for seating upholstery. Bark-tanned hides sourced from Swedish tanneries.
