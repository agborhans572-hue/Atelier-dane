Modern Slavery Act Statement

Financial year ending 31 December 2024 · Published pursuant to the Modern Slavery Act 2015

Our business and supply chains

Dane Design A/S is a Danish furniture and lighting company headquartered in Copenhagen, Denmark. We design and sell Scandinavian furniture through our website and showrooms. Our supply chain comprises twelve manufacturing workshops located in Denmark, Sweden, Norway, and Finland, all of which we visit and audit annually. We source materials — hardwood timber, natural textiles, leather, glass, and metals — exclusively from Scandinavian suppliers.

Our policies

We operate a Supplier Code of Conduct which all workshop and materials suppliers are required to sign as a condition of engagement. The Code prohibits forced labour, child labour, and all forms of modern slavery. It requires suppliers to pay at or above legal minimum wages and to maintain safe working conditions. Non-compliance results in immediate termination of the supply relationship.

Due diligence

Annual in-person audits are conducted at each of our twelve partner workshops by our production team. Audits include interviews with workers, inspection of payroll records, and review of health and safety documentation. We have found no evidence of modern slavery in our supply chain. We acknowledge that risks can evolve and commit to ongoing vigilance.

Training

All Dane Design employees who have direct responsibility for supply chain management receive annual training on identifying and reporting the signs of modern slavery. Our Supplier Code of Conduct is reviewed annually and updated to reflect best practice.
