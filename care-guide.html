Maintenance

Caring for Your Pieces

Natural materials reward a little attention. A few simple habits will keep your furniture looking considered for decades.

park

Solid Oak & Ash

Dust with a soft, dry cloth and wipe spills straightaway. Re-oil every six to twelve months to nourish the grain.

TipKeep out of direct sun and away from radiators to prevent drying and warping.

weekend

Leather

Dust weekly and condition twice a year with a natural balm. Blot spills gently — never rub.

TipA deepening patina is natural and part of the material’s character.

texture

Bouclé & Wool

Vacuum with an upholstery tool and spot-clean with a mild, diluted soap. Rotate cushions to wear evenly.

TipSnip — never pull — any loose loops with scissors to keep the weave intact.

build

Steel & Powder-Coat

Wipe with a damp cloth and dry immediately. Avoid abrasive cleaners that can dull the finish.

TipCheck and gently tighten any fixings once a year.

diamond

Marble & Stone

Use coasters and seal annually. Clean only with a pH-neutral solution and a soft cloth.

TipAcidic liquids like wine and citrus can etch — blot them up at once.

window

Glass

Clean with a streak-free spray and a microfibre cloth. Lift objects rather than dragging them.

TipFor fluted glass, work along the grooves to avoid trapping residue.

A few principles for every material

Beyond the specifics, three habits protect almost anything in your home.

Keep it steady

Aim for 40–60% humidity and avoid sudden temperature swings.

Soften contact

Use felt pads under objects and lift rather than slide.

Act quickly

Blot spills immediately; most marks are preventable, not permanent.

Designed to be repaired, not replaced

If a piece ever needs attention beyond home care, our workshop offers refinishing and spare parts.
